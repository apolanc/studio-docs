## Built for high-value, high-stakes conversations

### Access to Care and Scheduling

Automate the front door to care across booking, rescheduling, cancellations, reminders, and intake so patients can move forward faster and staff spend less time on repetitive coordination work.

### Medication Refill & Prescription Support

Guide patients through refill requests, renewal status checks, pharmacy selection, and medication follow-up with workflows that reduce delays and support adherence. Sensitive steps can be routed into the right review process without making patients start over.

### Pre-Visit Intake & Visit Readiness

Prepare patients for care by collecting forms, history, symptoms, and consent information before the visit, helping organizations reduce delays and improve operational readiness.

### Benefits,Coverage and Billing Guidance

Help patients understand eligibility, deductibles, prior requirements, and next steps using guided interactions grounded in approved healthcare content. This reduces confusion around coverage and helps patients move forward with fewer dropped journeys.

### Care Navigation & Provider Matching

Help patients find the right provider, service line, or next step based on symptoms, plan, location, and care context, turning healthcare navigation into a guided experience instead of a fragmented search task.

### Triage & Symptom Checking

Support symptom capture and structured next-step guidance that helps patients understand whether to self-serve, schedule, follow up, or escalate to clinical support.

### Care Coordination and Follow-up

Support post-visit follow-up, care instructions, status updates, and handoffs across channels so patients do not lose the thread between appointment, treatment, and next action. This is especially useful for ongoing or multi-step care journeys.

## Frequently asked questions

### What healthcare workflows can Rasa automate?

Rasa helps healthcare organizations automate common but high-friction workflows such as scheduling, intake, refills, navigation, and follow-up, reducing administrative burden while improving continuity for patients.

### Is Rasa suitable for regulated healthcare environments?

Yes. Rasa supports deployment in private cloud, on-prem, and hybrid environments, giving healthcare organizations stronger control over infrastructure, data handling, and governance.

### Can Rasa support both voice and digital healthcare experiences? 

Yes. Rasa enables one coordinated system across chat, voice, portal, app, and messaging channels, helping organizations deliver more continuous patient experiences.

### Can Rasa work with existing healthcare systems?

Yes. Rasa integrates with EHRs, scheduling platforms, patient portals, pharmacy systems, payer tools, and internal APIs through integrations and connectors.

### What healthcare organizations use Rasa?

Organizations such as Providence Health and nib Group use Rasa to support healthcare service delivery at scale. Providence used Rasa to power patient-facing experiences around scheduling and care navigation, while nib Group used Rasa to unify multiple service experiences into one more consistent member journey.

### How is Rasa different from other healthcare AI platforms?

Most healthcare bots either answer questions or route patients elsewhere. Rasa supports AI agents that can guide patients through real workflows across systems, while giving healthcare teams more control over deployment, integrations, and long-term ownership. It is built for organizations that need continuity, visibility, and adaptability in regulated environments.

### Can Rasa support multi-channel healthcare experiences?

Yes. Rasa enables one connected experience across chat, voice, patient portals, messaging, and mobile apps. Patients can move across channels without restarting from the beginning, and healthcare teams can extend the same workflows across touchpoints instead of maintaining separate logic in each one.

### How does Rasa handle patient triage and symptom checking?

Rasa supports structured symptom capture and next-step guidance that helps patients move to the appropriate level of care. Healthcare teams can define how follow-up questions, urgency checks, and escalation paths should work, creating guided experiences that are more consistent, reviewable, and operationally safe.
