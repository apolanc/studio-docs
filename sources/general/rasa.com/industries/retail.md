Retail customers do not think in channels or support categories. From pre-purchase guidance to post-purchase resolution, Rasa enables retailers to automate real customer work across chat, voice, apps, and messaging while keeping experiences consistent, brand-aligned, and operationally controlled.

## Built for high-value, high-stakes conversations

### Order Management & Post-Purchase Support

Automate the highest-volume moments after purchase, from order status and cancellation requests to delivery changes and shipment issues. This reduces “where is my order” volume while improving trust after purchase.

### Returns, Exchanges and Refund Workflows

Turn one of retail’s most expensive service categories into a smoother, more guided experience by helping customers start returns, check eligibility, choose exchange options, and track outcomes.

### Product Discovery & Shopping Guidance

Support shoppers before purchase with guided product search, comparison, fit or compatibility questions, and personalized recommendations that help move customers closer to checkout.

### Promotions & Checkout Recovery

Help customers understand offers, apply promo codes, resolve discount issues, and move through checkout-related questions without dropping out of the journey.

### Loyalty, Memberships and Repeat Purchase Support

Handle points balances, reward eligibility, subscription changes, and account-related service moments that influence retention and repeat revenue.

### Delivery Issues and Service Recovery

Respond to delivery failures, refund delays, replacement requests, and other moments where quick, consistent resolution matters most for customer trust.

### Support Triage & Escalation

When automation should hand off, the assistant collects the right details, summarizes the issue, and routes the case to the correct team with full context, including order information, timestamps, issue category, and urgency. Agents start with what they need. Customers do not have to repeat themselves. The handoff feels continuous, not disruptive.

## Frequently asked questions

### What retail workflows can Rasa automate?

Rasa can automate workflows such as order updates, returns, exchanges, refund status, product guidance, loyalty support, account service, delivery issue resolution, and escalation support across voice and digital channels.

### Can Rasa work with existing retail systems?

Yes. Rasa integrates with commerce, service, loyalty, payment, fulfillment, and CRM systems through APIs and connectors, allowing retailers to automate workflows without replacing core infrastructure.

### Can Rasa support both shopping and post-purchase experiences?

Yes. Rasa can support product discovery, promotions, checkout support, order management, returns, loyalty, and service recovery across the customer journey.

### Does Rasa support omnichannel retail experiences?

Yes. Rasa runs one assistant across web chat, mobile app, WhatsApp, SMS, and voice using a single logic layer. Shared memory and task state persist across touchpoints, so a customer who starts a return on chat and switches to WhatsApp picks up where they left off without repeating themselves.

### What retail companies use Rasa?

Enterprise retailers and e-commerce brands including Autodesk and Albert Heijn use Rasa to automate customer interactions across order management, post-purchase support, and delivery inquiries. Autodesk handles 100% of e-commerce webchat traffic with a 99.2% response rate. Albert Heijn improved delivery support quality by 37%.

### How is Rasa different from other conversational AI platforms for retail?

Rasa helps retailers automate real commerce and service workflows across systems, not just answer questions or route customers elsewhere. It gives teams more control over integrations, workflow behavior, and how experiences evolve across channels, which makes it a stronger fit for retailers that need flexibility, continuity, and long-term ownership.

### How does Rasa help product discovery and conversion?

Rasa helps shoppers move from questions to decisions by guiding product discovery, answering fit or compatibility questions, surfacing relevant offers, and connecting into catalog or commerce systems. This makes it easier for retailers to support assisted shopping experiences that reduce friction and move customers closer to checkout.
