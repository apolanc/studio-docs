Rasa Studio 2.0 is a visual, no-code interface that lets you build, test, and manage AI agents running on the Rasa platform. Whether you're a conversation designer shaping dialogue logic or a developer looking to speed up iteration cycles, Studio gives your entire team a shared workspace—no deep technical expertise required to get started.

## Who is Studio for?

Studio is built for any team that wants to deliver great conversational experiences without juggling raw configuration files every step of the way. Think of it as an advanced content management system for conversational AI: you work visually on the same assistant your engineering team deploys.

### Business users and conversation designers

You can bring your ideas to life directly in Studio. Craft business logic, fine-tune responses, and run tests—all without writing code. Studio keeps you focused on the conversation experience rather than implementation details.

### Technical teams

Studio adds a visual layer on top of your Rasa project, simplifying collaboration and shortening the feedback loop. You can export any assistant as YAML/code at any time, import existing Rasa-compatible projects, and keep full control over integration, customization, and deployment.

## How Studio relates to Rasa Pro

Studio runs **on top of** Rasa Pro, the underlying AI agent platform. Every flow you build in Studio compiles down to the same YAML structure used by Rasa Pro, which means:

- You can switch between the visual builder and raw YAML at any time.
- Custom actions, LLM configurations, and advanced pipeline settings live in `config.yml` and `endpoints.yml`, editable in **Assistant Settings → Configuration**.
- Deployments happen through Rasa Pro—Studio is the authoring surface.

<Note>
  To use Studio, you need a running Rasa Pro instance. Studio connects to it via the Studio URL you configure during installation.
</Note>

## CALM: the dialogue system underneath

Every agent you build in Studio is powered by **CALM** (Conversational AI with Language Models), Rasa's structured dialogue framework. CALM combines deterministic flows with LLM-driven natural language understanding, so your agents stay on track even when users take unexpected detours. Flows you design visually in Studio map directly to CALM primitives like `collect` steps, `logic` branches, and `link` steps.

Learn more about how CALM works: [CALM concepts →](/pro/concepts/calm)
